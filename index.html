Two ways to give

A single gift, two very different futures.

Imagine giving $10,000 to a cause you love. Where that gift lives determines how long it works for that cause.

One-time gift

Spent once.

A non-endowed gift is granted out in the year it's received. It does real good — feeds families, funds programs, supports neighbors today. But once it's spent, the work it does ends.

Endowed / Permanent fund

Working forever.

A permanent fund is invested in perpetuity. Each year, a portion is granted to the community; the rest stays invested and grows. The fund never gets spent — it makes grants every year, forever, while the principal continues to grow.
